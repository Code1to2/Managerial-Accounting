üìñ Section 1: Financial Management Fundamentals

üîë Key Concept: The Finance Function

Financial Management seeks to allocate financial resources through three primary activities:

1. üí∞ Financing Activities

  • Deciding which sources of funding to use
  • Determining optimal proportions of funding sources
  • Example: Should we issue shares or take a loan?

2. üìä Investing Activities

  • Deciding which investments to undertake
  • Working within available fund limitations
  • Example: Should we expand operations or develop new products?

3. üéØ Controlling Activities

  • Ensuring ethical implementation of financial decisions
  • Monitoring performance against targets
  • Example: Regular budget reviews and compliance checks

üìñ Section 2: Goals and Business Models

üîë Key Concept: Sustainable Value Creation

Primary Goal: Create and preserve long-term sustainable value for ALL stakeholders.

Evolution of Business Goals:

1990s Approach: Shareholder Wealth Maximization

  • Focus: Maximize profits for shareholders only
  • Problem: Short-term thinking, ignores other stakeholders

Modern Approach: Sustainable Value Creation

  • Focus: Balance all stakeholder interests
  • Considers: People, Profit, Planet (Triple Bottom Line)

üìñ Section 4: Risk and Capital Markets

üîë Key Concept: Types of Risk

Business Risk (Operating Risk):

  • Risk from normal business operations
  • Examples: Competition, demand changes, supply issues
  • Classification by risk level:
    • High Risk: Mining, Chemicals, Technology startups
    • Medium Risk: Restaurants, Manufacturing
    • Low Risk: Supermarkets, Utilities

Financial Risk (Leverage Risk):

  • Risk from using debt financing
  • Higher debt = Higher financial risk
  • Trade-off: Higher returns vs. Higher risk
Key Insight: Total Risk = Business Risk + Financial Risk
